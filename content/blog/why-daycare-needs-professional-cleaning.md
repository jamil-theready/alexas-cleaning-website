## Why Your Daycare Needs Professional Cleaning

Children touch everything. Floors, walls, toys, each other. A toddler will lick a table, sneeze on a shared book, and put a block in their mouth, all within 30 seconds. That is normal childhood behavior. It is also why daycares need a level of cleaning that goes far beyond what a standard commercial space requires.

If you run a daycare or childcare facility in Placerville or anywhere in El Dorado County, the cleanliness of your space is not just an operational concern. It is a trust issue. Parents are leaving their most precious people with you. They need to see and feel that your facility is safe.

## Children Are More Vulnerable to Germs

Young children have developing immune systems. They get sick more often and more easily than adults. The average toddler catches 8 to 12 colds per year. In a group care setting, that number can climb even higher because germs spread rapidly in enclosed spaces with shared surfaces.

The California Department of Social Services requires licensed childcare facilities to maintain specific sanitation standards. Meeting those standards consistently requires more than a quick wipe-down at the end of the day.

### High-Risk Surfaces in Daycares

- **Toys and play equipment** that multiple children handle daily
- **Changing stations** exposed to bodily fluids
- **Nap mats and bedding** that collect sweat, saliva, and skin cells
- **Kitchen and food prep areas** where snacks and meals are prepared
- **Bathroom fixtures** used by children still learning hygiene
- **Door handles and light switches** at child height

Every one of these surfaces needs daily attention with products that kill germs without leaving harmful residues behind.

## What Professional Daycare Cleaning Includes

### Daily Essentials

- Sanitize all toys and play surfaces
- Disinfect changing stations after each use (staff handles this, but professional end-of-day sanitization adds a safety layer)
- Clean and disinfect bathrooms completely
- Mop all floors with child-safe disinfectant
- Wipe down tables, chairs, and high chairs
- Empty all trash and diaper pails
- Clean kitchen and food prep surfaces
- Sanitize door handles, light switches, and railings

### Weekly Deep Cleaning

- Wash nap mats and bedding covers
- Deep clean carpets and rugs
- Scrub bathroom grout and tile
- Clean windows and window sills at child height
- Dust shelves, cubbies, and storage areas
- Wipe walls and baseboards where fingerprints accumulate

### Monthly Tasks

- Deep clean upholstered furniture
- Clean air vents and replace HVAC filters
- Sanitize all storage bins and containers
- Scrub outdoor play equipment if applicable
- Inspect and treat any mold or mildew in wet areas

## The Products Matter

This is critical. You cannot use the same cleaning products in a daycare that you would use in a warehouse or office building.

Children crawl on floors. They put toys in their mouths. They touch surfaces and then rub their eyes. Any chemical residue left behind becomes a direct exposure risk.

## Compliance and Licensing Requirements

California's Community Care Licensing Division conducts inspections of childcare facilities. Sanitation is a major component of those inspections. Violations can result in citations, fines, or even license suspension.

Professional cleaning helps you stay ahead of inspections rather than scrambling to prepare for them. When your facility is cleaned on a consistent professional schedule, you are always inspection-ready.

Key areas inspectors focus on include bathroom sanitation, food prep cleanliness, toy hygiene, and overall facility maintenance. A professional cleaning service documents their work, giving you records to reference during inspections.

## Parent Perception Is Everything

Parents tour your facility before enrolling their child. They look at the floors. They check the bathrooms. They notice the smell. A facility that looks and smells clean earns immediate trust. One that does not gets crossed off the list.

This is not superficial. Parents are making judgments about how well you care for the details. If the floors are sticky and the bathroom smells, they wonder what else is being overlooked. Fair or not, cleanliness is a proxy for quality of care.

Consistent professional cleaning signals to parents that you take every aspect of their child's environment seriously.

## Staff Burnout Is Real

Many daycare operators rely on staff to handle end-of-day cleaning. Teachers and caregivers already work long, physically demanding days. Adding deep cleaning to their responsibilities leads to burnout, shortcuts, and turnover.

Professional cleaning frees your staff to focus on what they do best: caring for children. It also ensures cleaning is done thoroughly and consistently, not rushed by someone who is exhausted at 6 PM after a full day with toddlers.
