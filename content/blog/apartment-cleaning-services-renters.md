## Why Apartment Cleaning Is Different

An apartment is not a house. And the cleaning approach should reflect that.

### Smaller Spaces, Higher Standards

Apartments have less square footage but more concentrated use. Your kitchen, living room, and dining area might share the same 400 square feet. Every surface works harder. Grease travels further. Dust builds up faster in tight spaces with less airflow.

### Landlord Expectations

Your landlord or property manager will inspect the unit when you leave. They are looking at the same things every time:

- Oven and stovetop condition
- Bathroom fixtures and grout
- Flooring (scuffs, stains, carpet wear)
- Walls (nail holes, marks, scuffs)
- Windows and blinds
- Closet interiors

The [California Department of Consumer Affairs](https://www.dca.ca.gov/) outlines tenant rights around security deposits. Your landlord can only deduct for damage beyond normal wear and tear. But "normal wear and tear" is subjective, and a dirty apartment gives them ammunition to keep more of your deposit.

### Shared Spaces

Many apartment complexes have rules about cleaning services accessing the property. Some require advance notice to management. Some restrict parking or access hours. Check with your leasing office before booking so there are no surprises on cleaning day.

Best for renters who want to maintain their space without spending weekends doing it. A biweekly cleaning for a 1-2 bedroom apartment typically runs $100 to $150 per visit.

### Move-In Cleaning

Moving into an apartment that is supposed to be clean but is not? Welcome to renting. Even units that have been "cleaned" by the property manager often have dust in cabinets, grime in bathroom grout, and mystery residue in the fridge.

A move-in clean gives you a fresh starting point. We document the condition of the unit when we clean it, which also protects you if there are disputes about the deposit when you move out later.

### Move-Out Cleaning

This is where we save people money. A proper move-out clean focuses on every area your landlord will inspect. We have a dedicated guide on [getting your deposit back](/blog/move-out-cleaning-checklist-deposit) that covers the full checklist.

#### Deposit Math

The average security deposit in El Dorado County for a 2-bedroom apartment is $1,500 to $2,500. A move-out deep clean costs $180 to $250. If a professional clean saves even half your deposit, you are coming out ahead.

## Tips for Renters Booking Cleaning Services

### Tell Your Property Manager

Not all complexes require it, but giving your management office a heads up avoids issues with gate access, parking, or noise complaints during cleaning hours.

### Point Out Pre-Existing Damage

Before your first clean, walk us through anything that was already there when you moved in. Stains on the carpet. Chips in the countertop. Scratches on the floor. We do not want to be blamed for something we did not cause, and neither do you.

### Schedule Before the Walkthrough

If you are doing a move-out clean, book us at least 3 days before your landlord inspection. This gives you time to do a final check and address anything that needs extra attention. Do not wait until the day before.

### Keep Clutter Minimal

We clean surfaces, not organize belongings. The less clutter on your counters, tables, and floors, the more thorough the cleaning. Pick up personal items before we arrive and we can focus on the actual cleaning.
