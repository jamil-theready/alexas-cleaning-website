When we walked in, the place was rough. Grease caked in the kitchen, pink mold in the shower, mystery stains on the carpet. The previous tenant clearly did not do a move-out clean.

## Why Turnover Cleaning Matters

Every day a unit sits empty is money lost. In El Dorado County, the average rent for a 2-bedroom is around $1,800 per month. That is $60 per day of vacancy.

A professional turnover clean that gets your unit rent-ready in 24 to 48 hours pays for itself immediately compared to a week of vacancy while you scramble to clean it yourself or wait for an available service.

### What Tenants Notice First

New tenants form their impression of the property in the first 5 minutes. These are the areas that set the tone:

- **Kitchen appliances.** A dirty oven or stained stovetop tells them the property is not well maintained.
- **Bathroom grout.** Discolored grout says "old." Clean grout says "cared for."
- **Floors.** Scuff marks, dust lines along walls, and dirty carpet give the impression nobody inspected the unit.
- **Smell.** Stale air, pet odor, or leftover cooking smells from the previous tenant are immediate turnoffs.

## The Turnover Cleaning Checklist

### Kitchen

- Oven interior and racks deep cleaned
- Stovetop and burner pans degreased
- Refrigerator cleaned inside and out (remove shelves and drawers)
- Microwave and dishwasher interiors wiped
- Countertops and backsplash scrubbed
- Sink and faucet polished
- Cabinet interiors and fronts wiped
- Floor mopped including under appliances

### Bathrooms

- Toilet disinfected (bowl, seat, base, behind)
- Shower or tub scrubbed including grout
- Glass doors or curtain hardware cleaned
- Vanity, sink, and mirror polished
- Cabinet interiors wiped
- Exhaust fan dusted
- Floor mopped

### All Rooms

- Floors vacuumed and mopped
- Baseboards wiped
- Light fixtures dusted
- Ceiling fans cleaned
- Window sills and tracks cleared
- Blinds dusted or wiped
- Closets wiped out
- Doors and frames spot-cleaned
- All hardware sanitized (switches, handles, knobs)

## Setting Up a Turnover System

If you manage multiple properties, ad hoc cleaning is not sustainable. Here is how to build a reliable turnover process.

### Have a Cleaning Service on Retainer

You do not need a contract. You need a relationship with a cleaning company that can respond quickly when a tenant moves out. At Alexa's Cleaning, several of our property management clients have us on a preferred vendor list. When a unit opens up, they call and we schedule within 48 hours.

### Create a Standard Inspection Checklist

Use the same checklist for every move-out inspection and every turnover clean. This keeps expectations consistent for tenants, cleaners, and new occupants. The [California Apartment Association](https://caanet.org/) offers resources for landlords managing rental properties in the state.

### Document Everything

Before and after photos protect both you and your tenants. We can provide documentation of unit condition after our cleaning, which supports fair deposit assessments.
